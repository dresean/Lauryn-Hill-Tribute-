Bio


Lauryn Noel Hill, born May 26, 1975, is an American lyricist, poet, singer, producer, actress, and performer. She gained early footing in the entertainment ciruit through film and television. A group she formed in her juvenile years quickly rose to fame with their sophomore album, The Score. The Fugees won several wows and accolades for their mastery of lyricism, eloquent production, and timeless positive messages juxtaposed to the painfully hypermasculine hip hop era. The members unfortunately split up and L. Boogie (Lauryn) went on to a strong solo career. The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill was Lauryn's debut album. It was MASSIVE. She later enlightened us all yet again with the renowned rhetorical epiphany, MTV Unplugged 2.0. The turning point in her career that lifted the superficial veil placed on the socioeconomic climate around us. She followed it up with an independently dropped EP Khulami Phase. Recently, Lauryn appeared on Nina Revisited: A Tribute To Nina Simone as the featured artist. She's adopted a reputation for dropping singles and other works without press or videos. She has been on an immaculately intimate world tour since 2013 with relatively no hiatus. Lauryn Hill, the martyr of sonic noteworthiness, shows no plans of stopping her salient mantra among an industry full of prodigious pabulum.
